The Bloch Sphere

Visualizing Single-Qubit States in 3D

Prerequisites - Understanding the Bloch Sphere

1. Qubit State Representation

Any pure qubit state can be written as |ψ⟩ = cos(θ/2)|0⟩ + e^(iφ)sin(θ/2)|1⟩, where θ ∈ [0,π] is the polar angle and φ ∈ [0,2π) is the azimuthal angle.

2. Why θ/2?

The factor of 2 comes from the relationship between SU(2) and SO(3). A 360° rotation in physical space corresponds to a 180° rotation on the Bloch sphere. This is the "spinor" property of spin-1/2 particles.

3. Cartesian Coordinates

The Bloch vector (x,y,z) relates to angles: x = sin(θ)cos(φ), y = sin(θ)sin(φ), z = cos(θ). The vector always has length 1 for pure states (lies on the sphere surface).

4. Special States

|0⟩ = North pole (z=1). |1⟩ = South pole (z=-1). |+⟩, |-⟩ = X-axis (±x). |i⟩, |-i⟩ = Y-axis (±y). Superpositions lie on the sphere surface between these points.

Bloch Sphere - The 4W+H Framework

WHAT is the Bloch Sphere?

The Bloch sphere is a geometric representation of a single qubit's state as a point on a unit sphere. Every pure state corresponds to a unique point on the surface. Mixed states (from decoherence) lie inside the sphere.

WHY use the Bloch Sphere?

It provides intuition! Quantum gates become rotations. Superposition is visualized as position. Measurement bases are axes. The abstract 2D complex Hilbert space becomes a tangible 3D object you can rotate and explore.

WHEN does the Bloch Sphere help?

Understanding single-qubit gates (rotations). Visualizing measurement (projection onto axis). Designing pulse sequences (paths on sphere). Teaching quantum mechanics. Debugging quantum circuits.

WHERE are the limitations?

Only works for SINGLE qubits! Multi-qubit states (especially entangled ones) cannot be visualized on a single Bloch sphere. Each qubit would need its own sphere, but entanglement means they're correlated in ways the spheres can't show.

HOW do gates act on the Bloch Sphere?

X, Y, Z gates = 180° rotations around their respective axes. H = 180° around (X+Z)/√2 axis. S, T = 90°, 45° rotations around Z. Rx(θ), Ry(θ), Rz(θ) = θ rotations around X, Y, Z axes.

From Angles to State Vector

The Bloch Sphere Parameterization

|ψ⟩ = cos(θ/2)|0⟩ + e^(iφ)sin(θ/2)|1⟩

θ controls the "latitude" (how much |0⟩ vs |1⟩). φ controls the "longitude" (relative phase between |0⟩ and |1⟩).

Example: |+⟩ state

θ = 90° = π/2, φ = 0°:

|+⟩ = cos(π/4)|0⟩ + e^(i·0)sin(π/4)|1⟩ = (1/√2)|0⟩ + (1/√2)|1⟩

Key Insight: Gates are Rotations

Every single-qubit gate is a rotation on the Bloch sphere! X rotates 180° around X-axis (swaps |0⟩↔|1⟩). Z rotates 180° around Z-axis (adds phase to |1⟩). H rotates 180° around the diagonal axis between X and Z. Understanding this makes quantum gates intuitive!
