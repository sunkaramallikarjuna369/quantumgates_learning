Multiple-Qubit Gates

Tensor Products and Multi-Qubit State Spaces

Overview

When we have multiple qubits, the combined state space grows exponentially. Two qubits live in a 4-dimensional space, three qubits in 8-dimensional, and n qubits in a 2n-dimensional space. This exponential growth is the source of quantum computing's power.

Product States vs Entangled States

Product States

Can be written as |ψ₁⟩ ⊗ |ψ₂⟩

  • |00⟩ = |0⟩ ⊗ |0⟩
  • |+0⟩ = |+⟩ ⊗ |0⟩
  • |+-⟩ = |+⟩ ⊗ |-⟩

Each qubit has its own independent state. Measuring one doesn't affect the other.

Entangled States

CANNOT be written as |ψ₁⟩ ⊗ |ψ₂⟩

  • |Φ⁺⟩ = (|00⟩ + |11⟩)/√2
  • |Φ⁻⟩ = (|00⟩ - |11⟩)/√2
  • |Ψ⁺⟩ = (|01⟩ + |10⟩)/√2
  • |Ψ⁻⟩ = (|01⟩ - |10⟩)/√2

Qubits are correlated. Measuring one instantly determines the other!

Key Concepts

Tensor Product

|ψ⟩ ⊗ |φ⟩

Combines individual qubit states into multi-qubit states.

Separability

|ψ⟩ = |ψ₁⟩ ⊗ |ψ₂⟩?

Product states can be factored; entangled states cannot.

Gate Extension

U ⊗ I

Apply gate U to first qubit, identity to second.

Exponential Growth

dim = 2n

State space doubles with each added qubit.
