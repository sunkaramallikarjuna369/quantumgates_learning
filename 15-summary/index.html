Universal Gate Sets

Common Universal Sets

  • Clifford + T: {H, S, CNOT, T} - Most common
  • CNOT + Rotations: {CNOT, Rx, Ry, Rz}
  • Toffoli + H: Minimal universal set

Solovay-Kitaev: Any gate can be approximated with O(log²(1/ε)) gates.

Key Takeaways

  1. Quantum gates are unitary matrices - They preserve probability and are reversible.
  2. Single-qubit gates are rotations on the Bloch sphere.
  3. Pauli gates (X, Y, Z) are π rotations around their respective axes.
  4. Hadamard creates superposition - The gateway to quantum parallelism.
  5. CNOT creates entanglement - Essential for quantum advantage.
  6. Universal gate sets can approximate any quantum operation.
  7. Gate decomposition breaks complex operations into simple gates.
  8. Bell states are maximally entangled and enable quantum protocols.
