Hadamard Gate

The Gateway to Superposition

Prerequisites

1. What is Superposition?

A qubit in superposition exists in a combination of |0⟩ and |1⟩ simultaneously. It's NOT "half 0, half 1" - it genuinely exists in both states until measured!

2. Measurement Probabilities

For |ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩, the probability of measuring 0 is |α|² and measuring 1 is |β|². The Hadamard creates equal 50/50 probabilities.

3. Bloch Sphere Equator

States on the equator have equal |0⟩ and |1⟩ probabilities. The Hadamard moves states between poles (|0⟩, |1⟩) and equator (|+⟩, |-⟩).

When to Use the Hadamard Gate

Create superposition from |0⟩: Apply H to get |+⟩ with 50/50 probabilities
Create superposition from |1⟩: Apply H to get |-⟩ with 50/50 probabilities
Return from superposition to basis: Apply H again (H² = I, self-inverse!)
Change X-basis to Z-basis: Apply H - it swaps X and Z bases
Start quantum algorithm: Apply H⊗n to n qubits for 2ⁿ superposition

Key Properties

Self-Inverse

H² = I

Applying H twice returns to original state

Hermitian

H† = H

Equal to its own conjugate transpose

Basis Swap

HXH = Z

Swaps X and Z bases

Rotation Axis

(X+Z)/√2

180° rotation about diagonal axis
