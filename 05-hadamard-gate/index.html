The Hadamard Gate

The Gateway to Superposition - The Most Important Single-Qubit Gate

Prerequisites - Understanding Superposition

1. What is Superposition?

A qubit in superposition is in multiple states simultaneously. |+⟩ = (|0⟩+|1⟩)/√2 means the qubit is equally |0⟩ and |1⟩ until measured. This is NOT uncertainty - it's a fundamental quantum property!

2. The Z-basis vs X-basis

Z-basis: {|0⟩, |1⟩} - the computational basis, eigenstates of Z. X-basis: {|+⟩, |-⟩} - superposition states, eigenstates of X. The Hadamard gate converts between these bases!

3. Normalization

The factor 1/√2 ensures probabilities sum to 1. For |+⟩ = (|0⟩+|1⟩)/√2: P(0) = |1/√2|² = 1/2, P(1) = |1/√2|² = 1/2. Total = 1.

4. Bloch Sphere Geometry

|0⟩ and |1⟩ are at the poles (Z-axis). |+⟩ and |-⟩ are on the equator (X-axis). H rotates 180° around the X+Z diagonal axis, swapping poles with equator!

Hadamard Gate - The 4W+H Framework

WHAT does Hadamard do?

The Hadamard gate creates superposition from basis states and vice versa. H|0⟩ = |+⟩ = (|0⟩+|1⟩)/√2 and H|1⟩ = |-⟩ = (|0⟩-|1⟩)/√2. It's a 180° rotation around the (X+Z)/√2 axis on the Bloch sphere.

WHY is Hadamard so important?

Hadamard is the gateway to quantum parallelism! By putting n qubits in superposition with H⊗n, you create 2ⁿ states simultaneously. This is how quantum algorithms explore exponentially many possibilities at once. Without H, quantum computers would just be classical computers!

WHEN to use Hadamard?

Start of algorithms: Create uniform superposition. Before measurement: Change measurement basis (H then measure = X-basis measurement). In oracles: Enable interference. Twice: HH = I, returns to original state.

WHERE in quantum circuits?

H gates typically appear at the beginning (initialization) and end (before measurement) of quantum circuits. In Grover's algorithm, H creates the initial superposition and is part of the diffusion operator. In QFT, H appears in the recursive structure.

HOW does the matrix work?

H = (1/√2)[[1,1],[1,-1]]. The 1/√2 normalizes. The pattern [1,1] and [1,-1] creates equal superposition with opposite phases. This matrix is both Hermitian (H=H†) and unitary (HH†=I), so H is its own inverse!

Hadamard as a Basis Change

H converts between the Z-basis (computational) and X-basis (Hadamard):

Input (Z-basis) → H → Output (X-basis)
|0⟩ = [1,0]ᵀ H |+⟩ = (|0⟩+|1⟩)/√2
|1⟩ = [0,1]ᵀ H |-⟩ = (|0⟩-|1⟩)/√2
Input (X-basis) → H → Output (Z-basis)
|+⟩ = (|0⟩+|1⟩)/√2 H |0⟩
|-⟩ = (|0⟩-|1⟩)/√2 H |1⟩

Key Insight: H swaps X and Z!

The Hadamard gate has a beautiful property: HXH = Z and HZH = X. This means if you "sandwich" an X gate between two H gates, you get a Z gate (and vice versa). This is because H rotates the Bloch sphere so that the X-axis becomes the Z-axis!

Important Hadamard Identities

Self-Inverse

HH = I (applying H twice returns to original)

Basis Swap

HXH = Z, HZH = X, HYH = -Y

Decomposition

H = (X + Z)/√2 (H is "between" X and Z)

Rotation Form

H = e^(iπ/2) · Rₓ₊ᵤ(π) = 180° rotation around (X+Z)/√2 axis

Hadamard in Quantum Algorithms

Creating Uniform Superposition

Apply H to each of n qubits starting in |0⟩⊗n:

H⊗n|0⟩⊗n = (1/√2ⁿ) Σₓ |x⟩ (sum over all 2ⁿ basis states)

This creates an equal superposition of ALL possible n-bit strings!

Grover's Algorithm

H creates initial superposition and is part of the diffusion operator:

Diffusion = H⊗n (2|0⟩⟨0| - I) H⊗n

Quantum Fourier Transform

QFT uses H gates combined with controlled rotations:

QFT = (H ⊗ I ⊗ ...) · (controlled rotations) · ...
