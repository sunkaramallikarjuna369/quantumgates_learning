Introduction to Quantum Gates

Building Blocks of Quantum Computation

Prerequisites - What You Should Know

1. Classical Bits

A classical bit can be either 0 or 1 - nothing in between. It's like a light switch: on or off.

2. Logic Gates

Classical gates (AND, OR, NOT) transform bits. For example, NOT flips 0→1 and 1→0. Quantum gates are similar but work on qubits!

3. Complex Numbers (Basic)

Quantum states use complex numbers (a + bi where i² = -1). Don't worry - we'll show you visually what this means!

The Bloch Sphere - Your Visual Guide

The Bloch sphere is a 3D representation where every point on the surface is a valid qubit state. This is your most important visualization tool!

Why Are Quantum Gates Special?

Property Classical Gates Quantum Gates
Reversibility Often irreversible (AND, OR) Always reversible
Information Can lose information Preserves all information
Representation Truth tables Unitary matrices
State Space Discrete (0 or 1) Continuous (Bloch sphere)

Quick Reference: Common Gate Effects

Flip |0⟩ ↔ |1⟩: Use X gate (quantum NOT)
Create superposition: Use H gate (Hadamard)
Add phase to |1⟩: Use Z gate or S/T gates
Rotate by any angle: Use Rx, Ry, Rz rotation gates
Entangle two qubits: Use CNOT (controlled-NOT)
